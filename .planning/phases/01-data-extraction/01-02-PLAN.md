```
Annual column anchors (1-indexed):
  col 4  = 2020 annual
  col 9  = 2021 annual  → cols 5-8  = 2021Q1..Q4
  col 14 = 2022 annual  → cols 10-13 = 2022Q1..Q4
  col 19 = 2023 annual  → cols 15-18 = 2023Q1..Q4
  col 24 = 2024 annual  → cols 20-23 = 2024Q1..Q4
  col 29 = 2025 annual  → cols 25-28 = 2025Q1..Q4  (MISLABELED as 2022Q1-Q4 in Excel!)
  col 34 = 2026 annual  → cols 30-33 = 2026Q1..Q4
  col 39 = 2027 annual  → cols 35-38 = 2027Q1..Q4
  col 44 = 2028 annual  → cols 40-43 = 2028Q1..Q4  (MISLABELED as 2027Q1-Q4!)
  col 49 = 2029 annual  → cols 45-48 = 2029Q1..Q4  (MISLABELED as 2027Q1-Q4!)
  col 54 = 2030 annual  → cols 50-53 = 2030Q1..Q4  (MISLABELED as 2027Q1-Q4!)
Build period_label_map BEFORE reading any data. DO NOT use ws.cell(row=X, col=Y).value for period labels.
```

**Sheet "Revenue Build" → `revenue-build.json`**

The sheet has 1407 rows × 56 columns. Most cells are None — only rows with segment revenue data are useful.

**Step 1: Build the correct period_label_map BEFORE reading any row data.**

Do NOT read period labels from the header row — they contain copy-paste errors per research. Instead, hardcode the positional mapping:

```python
ANNUAL_COL_ANCHORS = {4: 2020, 9: 2021, 14: 2022, 19: 2023, 24: 2024,
                       29: 2025, 34: 2026, 39: 2027, 44: 2028, 49: 2029, 54: 2030}
period_label_map = {}  # col_index -> label string
for anchor_col, year in ANNUAL_COL_ANCHORS.items():
    period_label_map[anchor_col] = str(year)
    if year > 2020:  # annual 2020 has no preceding quarters in range
        for q_offset, q_num in enumerate([1, 2, 3, 4], start=1):
            period_label_map[anchor_col - (5 - q_offset)] = f"{year}Q{q_num}"
# col 4 = "2020" (annual only), col 5 = "2021Q1", col 6 = "2021Q2", ...
```

**Step 2: Identify revenue rows.**

Segment revenue rows are identifiable by: label in column 1 OR column 2 contains a recognizable segment name, AND at least 4 non-None numeric values across the data columns (cols 4–54).

Key segments to extract (scan by label):
- "Total Revenue" or "Revenue" (consolidated)
- "US" or "AMC US" (US segment revenue)
- "International" or "Intl" (international)
- "Muvico" (if present as own row)
- Admissions, Food & Beverage (if present as distinct revenue rows)

For each qualifying row found, add it to the output with a camelCase key derived from the label.
