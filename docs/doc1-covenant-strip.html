What This Means

At this point, $475 million of these notes were still outstanding.

Recital 2 -- How They Got Permission to Amend
What This Means

Section 9.02 of the original Indenture allowed the company to change the bond contract if holders of at least 50%+ of the outstanding bonds agree. Critically, this includes consents gathered through a tender offer or exchange offer.

This is the legal mechanism AMC exploited: by bundling the consent solicitation with the Exchange Offer, AMC could get bondholders to vote to strip their own protections as a condition of exchanging into new, better-secured notes. Holders who did NOT participate in the exchange were left holding bonds with no covenants.

View Original Legal Text
WHEREAS, subject to certain exceptions, Section 9.02 of the Indenture provides, among other things, that the Company and the Trustee may modify or amend this Indenture or the Securities without notice to any Holder but with the written consent of the Holders of not less than a majority in aggregate principal amount of the Securities then outstanding voting as a single class (including consents obtained in connection with a tender offer or exchange offer for the Securities);
Recital 3 -- The Exchange Offer and Consent Solicitation
What This Means

AMC ran a two-part transaction:

  • Part 1 -- Exchange Offer: AMC offered bondholders the chance to swap their 6.125% unsecured notes for new 10%/12% Cash/PIK Toggle Second Lien Subordinated Secured Notes due 2026. The new notes carried a higher coupon and had actual collateral backing them -- a clear incentive to exchange.
  • Part 2 -- Consent Solicitation: As a condition of the exchange, participating holders had to vote to strip the covenants from the old notes they were leaving behind. This is the "exit consent" tactic -- you get a better deal, but only if you agree to make life worse for anyone who stays.
View Original Legal Text

Recital 4 -- Majority Consent Received
What This Means

AMC confirmed it received consent from holders of more than 50% of the $475 million in outstanding bonds, and delivered proof of that consent to the Trustee. Once the majority threshold was met, the amendment was binding on all holders -- including those who did not consent and did not exchange. This is the coercive element: minority holdouts had no way to block the strip.

View Original Legal Text

What This Means

AMC delivered two documents required by the original Indenture to make the amendment valid:

  • Officer's Certificate: A signed statement from an AMC officer confirming the amendment complies with the Indenture

Article I -- Amendments to Indenture and Securities
§ 1.1(a) Deletion of Protective Covenants (The Core Strip)
What This Means

This is the most destructive clause in the entire document. It deletes seven sections from the Indenture -- every single protective covenant that limited what AMC could do with bondholder money. Each deleted section and what it used to protect:

  • Section 4.05 -- Limitation on Consolidated Indebtedness: Previously capped how much total debt AMC could take on. Without this, AMC can borrow unlimited amounts senior to these notes.
  • Section 4.06 -- Limitation on Restricted Payments: Previously blocked AMC from paying dividends, buying back stock, or transferring value to equity holders while debt was outstanding. Without this, cash can flow to shareholders ahead of bondholders.
  • Section 4.07 -- Limitation on Liens: Previously restricted AMC's ability to pledge assets as collateral for other debt. Without this, AMC can encumber all assets with senior liens, pushing these bondholders further back in line.
  • Section 4.08 -- Limitation on Transactions with Affiliates: Previously prevented self-dealing -- AMC couldn't transfer assets to related parties at below-market prices. Without this, value can be moved away from bondholders.
  • Section 4.09 -- Limitation on Senior Subordinated Indebtedness: Previously restricted AMC from issuing more debt at the same level. Without this, these notes can be diluted by unlimited new same-priority debt.
  • Section 4.10 -- Future Guarantors: Previously required new subsidiaries to guarantee these notes. Without this, AMC can park assets in new subsidiaries beyond bondholders' reach.
  • Section 4.11 -- Change of Control: Previously required AMC to offer to repurchase the notes at 101% if there was a change of control. Without this, the company can be sold without giving bondholders an exit.

All seven sections are replaced with "[Intentionally Omitted]" -- legal language confirming they were deliberately erased, not accidentally forgotten.

View Original Legal Text
(a) The Indenture is hereby amended by deleting the following Sections of the Indenture and all references and definitions related solely thereto in their entirety: · Section 4.05 (Limitation on Consolidated Indebtedness); · Section 4.06 (Limitation on Restricted Payments); · Section 4.07 (Limitation on Liens); · Section 4.08 (Limitation on Transactions with Affiliates); · Section 4.09 (Limitation on Senior Subordinated Indebtedness); · Section 4.10 (Future Guarantors); · Section 4.11 (Change of Control); and All such deleted Sections are replaced with "[Intentionally Omitted]."
§ 1.1(b) Merger Covenant Gutted (Section 5.01)
What This Means

The original merger covenant (Section 5.01) had multiple conditions AMC had to meet before merging or consolidating with another entity. The revised version strips it down to only two requirements:

  • Obligation Assumption: If AMC merges into another entity, that entity must formally assume AMC's obligations under the notes (so there's still someone on the hook for the debt).
  • Officer's Certificate + Opinion of Counsel: AMC just needs to deliver a signed certificate from an officer and a lawyer's letter saying the merger complies. This is a self-certification -- essentially AMC vouching for itself.

What was removed: The original Section 5.01 likely required that after the merger, the surviving entity had to meet financial tests (e.g., no more leveraged than before), pass a "no default" test, and maintain certain coverage ratios. Those substantive protections are gone. Now AMC can merge with anything, as long as the new entity formally assumes the debt and AMC's own officer signs off.

View Original Legal Text

The Company shall not, in a single transaction or through a series of related transactions, consolidate with or merge with or into any other Person unless: (a) (i) either the Company shall be the continuing corporation or (ii) the Person formed by such consolidation or into which the Company is merged shall expressly assume, by an indenture supplemental hereto, executed and delivered to the Trustee all of the Obligations of the Company under the Securities and the Indenture; and (b) the Company has delivered, or cause to be delivered, to the Trustee, in form and substance reasonably satisfactory to the Trustee, an Officer's Certificate and an Opinion of Counsel, each stating that such consolidation or merger, and such supplemental indenture in respect thereto comply with the provisions described herein and that all conditions precedent herein provided for relating to such transaction have been complied with."
§ 1.1(c) Successor Substituted (Section 5.02)
What This Means

This is a conforming amendment to Section 5.02, matching it to the newly gutted Section 5.01. It establishes what happens after a permitted merger:

  • The surviving entity steps into AMC's shoes -- it gets all of AMC's rights and powers under the Indenture.
  • The old AMC is released from all obligations under the notes.

Combined with the weakened merger covenant in Section 5.01, this means AMC can effectively transfer itself into a new entity and walk away from the debt -- as long as the new entity signs the assumption agreement. There are no financial health requirements for the successor.

Successor Substituted. Upon any consolidation or merger, the successor corporation formed by such consolidation or into which the Company is merged, shall succeed to, shall be substituted for and may exercise every right and power of the Company under the Securities and this Indenture, with the same effect as if such successor corporation had been named as the Company herein. In the event of any transaction described and listed in Section 5.01 in which the Company is not the continuing corporation, the successor Person formed or remaining shall succeed to, be substituted for and may exercise every right and power of the Company, and the Company shall be discharged from all obligations and covenants under the Securities and this Indenture."
§ 1.1(d) Events of Default Gutted (Section 6.01)
What This Means

This is the second most damaging clause. It deletes Events of Default clauses (e) through (j) from Section 6.01. These were the tripwires that would let bondholders accelerate the debt (demand immediate full repayment) if AMC violated certain rules.

The deleted events of default likely included:

  • Cross-default: If AMC defaulted on other debt, these notes would also be in default
  • Judgment default: If a court entered a large judgment against AMC
  • Covenant breach default: If AMC violated any of the covenants (which are now deleted anyway)
  • Guarantee invalidity: If the subsidiary guarantees became unenforceable
  • Bankruptcy of subsidiaries: If key subsidiaries entered insolvency

After this deletion, the remaining Events of Default are likely only: (a) failure to pay interest, (b) failure to pay principal at maturity, (c) failure to comply with remaining covenants after notice, and (d) bankruptcy of the Company itself. AMC can default on every other obligation, have subsidiaries collapse, and lose lawsuits -- and these bondholders cannot accelerate.

View Original Legal Text
(d) Clauses (e), (f), (g), (h), (i) and (j) of Section 6.01 (Events of Default) are hereby deleted in their entirety and replaced with "[Intentionally Omitted]," and all references in the Indenture to the clauses so eliminated are deleted in their entirety.
§ 1.1(e) Guarantor Merger and Release Rules (Section 11.03(b))
What This Means

This rewrites the rules for when Guarantors (AMC's subsidiaries that backstop the debt) can merge with other entities. The amended version:

  • If a Guarantor merges with an outside party, the surviving entity must assume the guarantee obligations (or the guarantee terminates).
  • Requires only an Officer's Certificate and Opinion of Counsel -- again, self-certification.
  • Automatic release clause: If a Guarantor is sold or merged with any entity that is NOT AMC or an AMC subsidiary, that Guarantor's guarantee automatically terminates.

This makes it trivially easy to release subsidiary guarantees through corporate transactions -- sell the subsidiary, and the guarantee vanishes. Since the covenant requiring future subsidiaries to become guarantors (Section 4.10) was also deleted, no new guarantees will ever be added.

§ 1.2 Release of All Subsidiary Guarantees
What This Means

Every single subsidiary guarantee is immediately released. Before this amendment, AMC's subsidiaries were on the hook alongside the parent company -- if AMC couldn't pay, bondholders could go after subsidiary assets. After this one sentence, that backstop is gone.

This matters enormously because AMC's subsidiaries hold most of the actual value -- the theater leases, the real estate interests, the operating businesses. The parent company (AMC Entertainment Holdings) is primarily a holding company. By releasing the Guarantors, this amendment effectively makes these notes a claim against a shell with limited direct assets.

§ 2.8 The Trustee's Role and Disclaimers
What This Means

This is the Trustee's liability shield. Key points:

  • The Trustee agrees to perform its duties under the amended Indenture, but only on the same terms and protections it had before -- including all limits on its liability.
  • The Trustee makes no representation about whether this amendment is valid, fair, or proper. It's not vouching for the Exchange Offer, the consent process, or any of the recitals in the document.
  • The Trustee takes no responsibility for the consent solicitation materials or the exchange offer documents.

In plain terms: the Trustee is saying "AMC told us the votes were there, we're signing this because the procedures were followed, but don't look to us if something was wrong." This is standard for trustees -- they are ministerial agents, not fiduciaries who independently protect bondholders.

§ 2.9 Effectiveness and Timing
What This Means

This clause has a crucial two-step timing mechanism:

  • The amendment itself is effective immediately upon signing on July 27, 2020.
  • But the actual covenant changes (Article I) only kick in when the Exchange Offer is consummated -- meaning when the new 10%/12% PIK Toggle notes are actually issued and the old notes surrendered.

This protects AMC: if the exchange fell through, the covenant strip wouldn't take effect. But once the exchange closed, the strip became permanent and irrevocable. AMC was required to notify the Trustee when the exchange was completed (or if it was abandoned).

AMC also represents and certifies to the Trustee that the majority consent threshold was met. This is another self-certification -- the Trustee relies on AMC's word rather than independently counting votes.

Impact Summary
What This Document Did to Creditors
Critical Impact Assessment

This four-page document represents one of the most aggressive covenant-stripping transactions in the history of high-yield debt. Here is the total damage:

Covenants Deleted (7 of 7):

  • Limitation on Indebtedness -- AMC can now borrow unlimited amounts ahead of these notes
  • Restricted Payments -- Cash can flow to equity holders at will
  • Limitation on Liens -- All assets can be pledged to other creditors
  • Affiliate Transactions -- Self-dealing is unrestricted
  • Senior Subordinated Debt Limitation -- Unlimited dilution at this level
  • Future Guarantors -- No new subsidiaries will ever guarantee these notes
  • Change of Control -- No put right if the company is sold

Events of Default Deleted (6 of ~10):

  • Cross-default, judgment default, guarantee invalidity, subsidiary bankruptcy, and covenant breach triggers -- all gone
  • Only payment default and company-level bankruptcy remain as triggers

Guarantees Released:

  • All subsidiary guarantees immediately terminated
  • These notes went from a claim against the entire AMC enterprise to an unsecured, unguaranteed claim against the holding company

Merger Covenant Gutted:

  • AMC can merge into any entity with only self-certification, no financial health tests

Strategic Purpose: This cleared the path for AMC's 2020 restructuring. The exchanging holders got new 10%/12% Second Lien Secured Notes with actual collateral. The holdouts were left with a covenant-free, guarantee-free unsecured instrument against a company in severe financial distress during COVID-19. This is the textbook "exit consent" playbook.
