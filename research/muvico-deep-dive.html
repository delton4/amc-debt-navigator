2.2The Drop-Down Mechanics (July 22, 2024 — LME 1.0)

AMC executed a J.Crew-style liability management exercise:

  1. Created Centertainment Development, LLC as a wholly-owned subsidiary of AMC
  2. Created Muvico, LLC as a wholly-owned subsidiary of Centertainment
  3. Transferred 175 domestic theaters (representing ~53% of domestic LTM EBITDA) plus the AMC brand/IP to Muvico via the Asset Transfer Agreement
  4. Issued new debt at Muvico — $2B Term Loan (DOC 4) + $414M Exchangeable Notes (DOC 7) — secured by the transferred assets
  5. AMC Group Obligors remain guarantors on all four instruments, but the Muvico assets are now directly pledged to Muvico-level creditors with priority
2.3The July 2025 Settlement (LME 2.0)

Left-behind AMC 7.5% First Lien Noteholders (DOC 3) sued in September 2024. Settlement in July 2025: - ~62% of 7.5% noteholders provided ~$244M new money + exchanged $590M existing notes - Received $857M of new Muvico 15% PIK Notes (DOC 2) — 1.5L on Muvico, 1L on AMC - Also created DOC 5 (new 8% PIK Exchangeable Notes, $154.5M) as part of the same transaction - ~90% of Term Loan holders provided consent to the amendment

Restructuring Implications

The drop-down created structural subordination for any AMC-only creditors (DOC 3, DOC 1). Muvico creditors have direct claims on the 175 theaters and IP. AMC-only creditors must pierce through AMC’s equity interest in Centertainment to reach those assets — a claim that is structurally junior to all Muvico-level debt.

This tension between entity-level claims is the central restructuring battleground.

Restructuring Implications

The Soft Call on DOC 7 is effectively impossible at current stock prices — AMC trades at ~$1.25 and DOC 7 requires $7.924+/share for 15 consecutive days. However, DOC 5’s Soft Call is potentially achievable: at the Exchange Price floor ($1.50), the trigger is only $1.65/share — just 32% above current market. If AMC stock recovers modestly, the company could call DOC 5 at par, eliminating $154.5M of 1.25L debt. This asymmetry between DOC 5 and DOC 7 Soft Call triggers is a critical strategic consideration. The make-whole on DOC 2 makes early redemption expensive. The Special Mandatory Redemption in both DOC 5 (Section 3.08) and DOC 7 (Section 3.07) creates a potential cliff: if AMC cannot reduce the 2029 First Lien Notes below $190M by November 2028, holders can force mandatory redemption. This interacts with the Term Loan’s own Springing Maturity Condition (same $190M threshold).

6.2Key Differences

DOC 2: Uses a 40% voting stock threshold (more sensitive — a smaller stake triggers it). Change of Control defined as either (a) 40%+ person/group acquiring voting stock of AMC or (b) Company ceasing to be wholly-owned subsidiary of AMC.

DOC 5 / DOC 7: Use a 50% voting stock threshold plus additional triggers: (c) stockholder approval of liquidation/dissolution and (d) Common Stock ceases to be listed on NYSE/NASDAQ. Restructuring Implications

A sale of AMC or Muvico theaters would trigger Change of Control across all instruments. The 40% threshold in DOC 2 is more easily triggered than the 50% threshold in DOC 5/7 — meaning a partial sale or strategic investor could trigger DOC 2 without triggering DOC 5/7. Any delisting of AMC common stock triggers Fundamental Change under DOC 5/7, giving holders a par put — relevant if AMC’s equity becomes worthless and the company considers going dark.

10. Permitted Liens
10.1DOC 7 Framework (Section 4.07)

Three-part structure: - Section 4.07(a): No Liens (other than Permitted Liens) on Collateral - Section 4.07(b): No Liens on non-Collateral assets unless Notes are equally and ratably secured (or, for Junior Indebtedness under §4.05(b)(xix), on senior basis) - Section 4.07(c): Any equal-and-ratable Lien for Holders auto-releases when Initial Lien releases or becomes Permitted Lien

10.2Key Observations

The lien covenant interacts with the multi-tiered ICA structure. Permitted Liens include: - Liens securing the Term Loan (1L) - Liens securing the Exchangeable Notes (1.25L per DOC 5; 2L per DOC 7) - Liens securing the 15% PIK Notes (1.5L) - Liens for Indebtedness permitted under Section 4.05(b)(viii) — First Lien Priority or lesser

The equal-and-ratable mechanic in Section 4.07(b) means that if Muvico grants a lien on any non-Collateral asset, the Notes automatically get an equivalent lien — this prevents the company from creating off-collateral security arrangements that favor one creditor over another.

Restructuring Implications

The permitted liens framework is tightly drafted and prevents Muvico from granting priming liens without navigating the amendment/sacred rights provisions. The cross-default cascade is the most dangerous feature. The Exchange Adjustment make-whole on DOC 7 acceleration adds ~$19.3M in potential claims, and DOC 5’s higher Exchange Adjustment (21% vs 18%) adds proportionally more on its $154.5M face.

19. Corporate Separateness
19.1The Five Requirements (DOC 5, Section 4.20; DOC 7, Section 4.20)

Section 4.20 — Muvico Group Entities Corporate Separateness:

# Requirement Detail
(a) Enforce Intercompany Agreement rights Against applicable counterparties in a timely and diligent manner
(b) Maintain concentration/collection accounts At US banks with ≥$5B combined capital and surplus; subject to springing account control agreements perfecting Notes Collateral Agent’s Lien; Muvico-earned amounts must be deposited and retained
(c) Observe all corporate formalities In all material respects
(d) Maintain separate books and accounts For all Muvico Group Entities; including recording amounts under Intercompany Agreements and distributions to parent entities
(e) Maintain separate assets From any Person other than Muvico Group Entities; except as permitted by Intercompany Agreements
19.2Why This Matters

Corporate Separateness provisions serve two critical functions:

  1. Prevent Substantive Consolidation in Bankruptcy: If AMC files Chapter 11, creditors might argue that AMC and Muvico should be substantively consolidated (treated as a single entity for claims purposes). Substantive consolidation would eliminate the Muvico priority waterfall and pool all claims together. The Corporate Separateness covenant is designed to create a factual record that Muvico operates independently — separate accounts, separate books, separate formalities — making it harder for a court to order consolidation.

  2. Protect Collateral Value: The concentration account requirement with springing control ensures that Muvico’s cash flows are traceable and pledged. The requirement to deposit and retain earnings in controlled accounts prevents AMC from commingling Muvico cash flows, which would dilute the collateral package.

19.3Account Control Specifics

The bank must have combined capital and surplus of at least $5,000,000,000 — this limits accounts to major US banks (JPMorgan, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, Citigroup, etc.) and ensures institutional reliability. The springing account control agreement provides perfection of the Lien but does not give the Notes Collateral Agent actual account control pre-default — it “springs” into full control upon an Event of Default.

Restructuring Implications

These provisions are the creditors’ shield against substantive consolidation. If AMC and Muvico are substantively consolidated, the multi-tiered lien priority becomes meaningless — all claims pool together. Muvico’s compliance (or non-compliance) with these five requirements will be a critical factual question in any bankruptcy proceeding. Term Loan holders, who are 1L on both AMC and Muvico, might actually prefer consolidation (larger asset base, no entity-level waterfall). Junior Muvico creditors (DOC 5/2/7) would resist consolidation because it would eliminate their priority over AMC-only creditors on Muvico assets.

Restructuring Implications

Articles IV-A and IV-B create a “covenant cage” around the entire corporate structure. Holdings cannot do anything except hold Muvico stock. AMC Group Obligors are bound by the Term Loan covenants even through the DOC 5/7 indentures (Article IV-B incorporation). UK Holdco cannot dispose of AMC UK stock. The absolute prohibition on Holdings disposing of Muvico equity (Article IV-A(e)) means the Centertainment structure cannot be unwound without creditor consent — AMC cannot sell Muvico to a third party through Holdings without violating this covenant. Any restructuring that involves a sale, merger, or entity-level rearrangement must navigate all five covenant layers simultaneously.
