2. Why Odeon 12.750% Notes Likely Trade Above Par
2.1Coupon Premium vs. Market Rates
  • A 12.750% fixed coupon is exceptionally high for senior secured paper. Even in the elevated rate environment of 2023-2025, comparable European high-yield secured notes typically carry coupons in the 6-9% range.
  • This coupon was set at issuance in October 2022, during peak rate uncertainty and distressed cinema-sector sentiment. It reflects the risk premium demanded at that moment.
  • As box office recovery materialized and cinema-sector fundamentals stabilized, the risk premium embedded in the coupon became excessive relative to actual performance — driving the price above par.
2.2Post-COVID Cinema Recovery
  • Global box office recovered significantly in 2023-2024, with major tentpole releases (Barbie, Oppenheimer, etc.) driving attendance.
  • Odeon operates across UK, Nordics (Filmstaden), Germany (UCI), Spain (Cinesa), and Finland (Finnkino) — diversified European exposure that benefited from this recovery.
  • Improved EBITDA and cash flow generation reduced credit risk below what was priced at issuance.
2.3Call Protection / Make-Whole
  • If the indenture includes standard non-call provisions with a make-whole premium (typical for high-yield notes), the issuer cannot refinance cheaply. This protects investors’ above-market coupon stream.
  • The Fixed Charge Coverage Ratio test steps up from 1.50x (through Dec 2023) to 2.00x (after), combined with a First Lien Leverage Ratio cap of 3.00x, indicating the structure was designed for a recovering credit.
2.4Structural Seniority
  • These notes sit at the top of the capital structure as first-lien senior secured.
  • Collateral package includes shares, IP (Odeon, Filmstaden, UCI, Cinesa, Finnkino brands), bank accounts, receivables, insurance, and floating charges over UK/Finnish entity assets.
  • The Intercreditor Agreement explicitly provides for ranking priority: Senior Secured > Second Lien > Senior Unsecured > Investor Liabilities.
2.5AMC Strategic Value
  • Odeon is AMC’s entire European operation. A sale of Odeon has been periodically rumored, which could trigger a change-of-control put or early redemption at premium prices.
  • AMC’s own deleveraging efforts (APE conversion, equity raises) have indirectly improved the credit profile of subsidiaries.
3. UK Security Agent Convention
3.1What Is a Security Agent?

In UK/European leveraged finance, the Security Agent is the entity that holds security (collateral) on behalf of all secured creditors. This contrasts with the US model:

Feature UK Security Agent US Collateral Agent/Trustee
Role Ministerial/administrative only Fiduciary duties under TIA
Fiduciary Duty Explicitly disclaimed (no fiduciary duties to secured parties) Fiduciary duties to noteholders/lenders
Authority Acts only on instructions from ICA-defined “Instructing Group” Acts per indenture terms, more independent discretion
Conflict Resolution ICA controls; security docs defer to ICA in all conflicts Indenture terms control
Liability Liability limited to gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud Higher standard of care
Delegation Can freely appoint delegates, co-agents, attorneys More restricted delegation
Resignation Can resign with notice; must appoint successor Regulated by indenture/TIA
3.2Key Provisions from the Odeon Indenture (Section 12.07)

The Odeon indenture’s Security Agent provisions are textbook UK-style:

  1. Ministerial Only: The Security Agent acts “solely in a ministerial and administrative capacity” — it has NO obligation to monitor compliance, no duty to investigate defaults, and no independent judgment role.

  2. No Fiduciary Duties: Expressly stated that the Security Agent owes no fiduciary duties to any Holder or other secured party.

  3. ICA Controls: In case of any conflict between the Indenture and the Intercreditor Agreement, the ICA prevails. The Security Agent follows ICA instructions.

  4. Instructing Group Directs: The Security Agent acts on directions from the “Instructing Group” (defined in the ICA — typically a majority of senior secured creditors by exposure). It has no independent enforcement discretion.

  5. Co-Agents & Delegates: The Security Agent may appoint any number of co-security agents, sub-agents, or attorneys — common in multi-jurisdictional European structures where local agents are needed.

  6. Indemnification: The Security Agent is entitled to full indemnification before taking any action. It can refuse to act if not indemnified to its satisfaction.

  7. No Liability: Not liable for any loss unless caused by its own gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud. Not liable for any delegate’s acts.

  8. Dual Role: U.S. Bank serves as BOTH Trustee (US-style role for noteholder relations) AND Security Agent (UK-style role for collateral). This dual structure is common in cross-border deals.

3.3Why This Matters for Restructuring

For a lender considering an LME: - The Security Agent cannot independently block a transaction — it follows ICA instructions. - The Instructing Group threshold determines who controls enforcement. Capturing this majority is critical. - The Security Agent’s ministerial role means it will execute whatever the Instructing Group directs, including releasing collateral, consenting to amendments, or enforcing — making the ICA the true battleground document. - Any creditor-on-creditor violence must navigate the ICA’s consent thresholds, not just the indenture’s.

4. Agreed Security Principles (Exhibit D) — Full Analysis
4.1What Are Agreed Security Principles?

Agreed Security Principles (“ASPs”) are a uniquely European/UK concept in leveraged finance. They are a detailed exhibit attached to credit agreements and indentures that define the parameters, limitations, and practical boundaries of the security package across multiple jurisdictions.

Why they exist: European leveraged deals involve entities across many jurisdictions (UK, Germany, Spain, Finland, Sweden, Italy, etc.), each with different corporate law, registration requirements, tax rules, and practical limitations on granting security. The ASPs prevent the security package from becoming impossibly expensive or legally problematic.

4.2The Overriding Principle

The ASPs establish an “Overriding Principle” — security is ONLY required as specifically described:

By Jurisdiction:

Jurisdiction Security Granted
England & Wales Fixed charge over shares, IP (Odeon brand), receivables, bank accounts, intercompany receivables, hedging agreements, licenses, goodwill, uncalled capital; Assignment of material insurance policies; Floating charge over all assets
Finland Shares, intercompany receivables, bank accounts (no control pre-default); Floating charge over all assets; IP (Finnkino brand); Business mortgage promissory note to Finnish Patent Office
Germany Shares (UCI brand entities), intercompany receivables, bank accounts; IP (UCI brand); Share pledges must be notarized by German notary
Spain Shares (Cinesa), intercompany receivables, bank accounts (control only after Declared Default); All Spanish docs must be notarized by Spanish notary
Sweden Shares (NCG Holding AB, Filmstaden AB, Filmstaden Media AB), intercompany receivables, bank accounts (explicitly acknowledged security NOT perfected); IP (Filmstaden brand)

No security required from: - Entities in “Excluded Jurisdictions” - Joint ventures or minority-owned entities - Non-wholly-owned subsidiaries - Irish entities (explicitly carved out of certain obligations)

4.3Key Limitations & Carve-outs

The ASPs contain extensive debtor-friendly limitations:

  1. Corporate Benefit / Financial Assistance: Guarantees and security limited to the maximum amount permissible under local corporate law — including capital maintenance rules, financial assistance prohibitions, “thin capitalization” rules, and fiduciary duties of directors. (Para 2(a))

  2. Cost-Benefit Test: Security NOT required if legal fees, registration fees, stamp duty, or taxes are “disproportionate to the benefit obtained by the Finance Parties.” (Para 2(c))

  3. Materiality Threshold: Only “material assets” in a category need be secured if securing all assets would be costly. (Para 2(d))

  4. Third-Party Consent Barriers: Assets subject to contractual restrictions (leases, licenses, JV agreements, cash pools) are excluded if charging them would give a third party termination rights. Obligors must use “reasonable efforts” for 45 days to obtain consent, then the obligation ceases. (Para 2(e))

  5. Legal Capacity: No security if it would be outside the Obligor’s legal capacity, result in a void document, or create personal liability for directors/officers. (Para 2(f))

  6. Unduly Burdensome: No security if it would “restrict the ability of the relevant member of the Group to conduct its operations and business in the ordinary course.” (Para 2(g))

  7. German Tax Protection: No security that would trigger German non-resident tax liability or withholding tax deduction for lenders. (Para 2(p))

  8. No Real Estate: Obligors are NOT required to grant security over real estate (though it may be captured by floating charges). No title investigation, surveys, or environmental due diligence required. (Para 12)

4.4Enforcement Mechanics — “Declared Default” Concept

A critical feature: Security is NOT enforceable until a “Declared Default” occurs, meaning: - Acceleration notice under Clause 23.16, OR - Automatic acceleration

Pre-Declared Default: - No claims under guarantees - No enforcement of security - No notices to third parties - No exercise of powers of attorney - Pledgors retain all voting rights on pledged shares - Obligors retain full control of bank accounts - No loss payee annotations on insurance - No notice of security to IP licensors - Free dealing with receivables, contracts, and all secured assets

Post-Declared Default: - Full enforcement rights activate - Security Agent can exercise power of attorney - Share voting rights may transfer (except German shares — pledgor retains always) - Bank account control activates (including Spain) - Notice requirements to third parties trigger

4.5Implications for LME Strategy

For a lender considering an LME on Odeon:

  1. The ASPs are a shield: The extensive carve-outs mean the actual security package is narrower than it appears. A lender must understand exactly what collateral is actually perfected and enforceable.

  2. Multi-jurisdictional complexity: Any enforcement must navigate 6+ jurisdictions simultaneously. The ASPs acknowledge this is impractical — which is why European restructurings tend toward consensual solutions.

  3. Floating charges are powerful but subordinate: In UK insolvency, floating charges crystallize but rank behind fixed charges and preferential creditors. The prescribed part (a portion set aside for unsecured creditors) further dilutes recovery.

  4. Declared Default is a binary switch: Until acceleration, secured creditors have almost no enforcement power. This creates significant negotiating dynamics — the threat of acceleration is the leverage, not the actual security.

  5. Cost provisions favor the borrower: The ASPs’ cost-benefit tests give the company significant discretion to argue that perfecting or enforcing security is “disproportionate” — a litigation hook in any enforcement scenario.

5.4Super Senior Designation Date

The ICA distinguishes between enforcement before and after a “Super Senior Designation Date” — a mechanism allowing revolving/working capital facilities to have super-priority in enforcement proceeds. This is standard in European leveraged finance and affects recovery waterfalls significantly.

5.5Enforcement Principles (Schedule 4)

The ICA contains detailed “Enforcement Principles” that govern HOW security can be enforced: - Must use “Competitive Process” (public/private auction with multiple bidders) - Advised by “reputable internationally recognised investment bank” - Instructing Group controls the process - Proceeds distributed per the waterfall

5.6Distressed Disposal Mechanics

The ICA defines “Distressed Disposal” — disposal of Group assets when: - Requested by Instructing Group after security becomes enforceable - Effected by enforcement of Transaction Security - After a Distress Event, by a Debtor to non-Group purchaser

This is the mechanism by which a sale of Odeon’s business (or parts of it) could be forced in a restructuring.

7. Restructuring Implications for Lenders
7.1Leverage Points

  1. Control the Instructing Group: The ICA defines the Instructing Group threshold. Accumulating a majority of senior secured exposure = controlling enforcement decisions.

  2. Declared Default as Leverage: The binary nature of the Declared Default means threatening acceleration is the primary tool. Pre-default, the security package is largely dormant.

  3. Multi-jurisdictional Complexity Favors Negotiation: The ASPs acknowledge that cross-border enforcement is difficult and expensive. This inherently favors consensual restructuring over enforcement.

  4. AMC Parent Has Limited Direct Exposure: AMC UK Holding Limited’s security is limited to share pledges. AMC Entertainment Holdings (US) is not a guarantor. Lenders’ recourse is primarily to European assets.

7.2Potential LME Approaches

Given the above-par trading and strong coupon:

  1. Consent Solicitation: Seek covenant amendments in exchange for a consent fee. The above-par trading suggests holders are comfortable — making consent solicitations feasible.

  2. Tender Offer: Issuer could offer to repurchase notes at a premium to par, funded by lower-cost refinancing. The 12.750% coupon creates strong economic incentive to refinance.

  3. Exchange Offer: Offer new notes with longer maturity or different terms in exchange for existing notes. The approaching 2027 maturity creates a natural refinancing event.

  4. Drop-Down / Asset Transfer: The structural ring-fencing and ASP carve-outs create potential pathways for asset transfers outside the secured perimeter — though the ICA’s Distressed Disposal provisions and covenant restrictions would constrain this.

7.3Defensive Considerations

For lenders protecting their position:

  1. Monitor ICA Voting Thresholds: Know exactly what constitutes the Instructing Group and track holdings.
  2. Understand ASP Limitations: The actual collateral is narrower than headline descriptions suggest.
  3. Watch for Restricted Payments: Upstream distributions to AMC could leak value.
  4. Track Cross-Jurisdictional Risks: Different enforcement timelines and costs across 6+ jurisdictions.
  5. Cooperation Agreements: Consider coordinating with other noteholders to maintain blocking positions on key amendments.

Implication: Any acceleration or bankruptcy filing triggers the applicable call premium. Currently 103.188%; drops to par after November 1, 2026. This means an AMC Ch.11 filing before November 2026 would trigger a ~$12.8M premium ($400M × 3.188%) on top of par.

10. Change of Control — Put Right & Drag-Along
10.1Change of Control Definition

“Change of Control” means any of the following after the Issue Date:

(a) Any person/group (other than Permitted Holders) becomes beneficial owner of 50% or more of the total voting power of the Voting Stock of AMC or the Company (Odeon Cinemas Group Limited);

(b) Adoption of a plan relating to the liquidation or dissolution of AMC or the Company;

(c) The sale/transfer of all or substantially all of the assets of AMC and its Subsidiaries (taken as a whole) or the Company and its Subsidiaries (taken as a whole) to any Person other than Permitted Holders.

Permitted Holders: AMC and its Restricted Subsidiaries, Management Investors and their Permitted Transferees, and any “group” of which they are members (provided no other person in the group owns 50%+ of voting stock).

Key insight: A Change of Control at the AMC parent level triggers a put right at Odeon. A hostile takeover of AMC = CoC at Odeon.

10.2Put Right Mechanics (Section 4.12)
  • Purchase price: 101% of principal + accrued interest + Additional Amounts
  • Notice: Within 60 days of the CoC, Issuer must notify all Holders
  • Payment Date: 20-60 Business Days from notice
  • Withdrawal right: Holders may withdraw up to 10 Business Days after notice
  • Min denomination: Unpurchased portion must be at least $200,000
  • Concurrent consent solicitation: Permitted, but tender cannot be conditioned on delivering consents
  • Price flexibility: Issuer may increase the CoC Payment above 101%
10.3Squeeze-Out / Drag-Along (Section 4.12)

If Holders of 90% or more validly tender and do not withdraw, the Issuer has the right to redeem all remaining outstanding Notes at the CoC Payment price (101%+) with 10-60 days’ notice. This is a drag-along provision — prevents holdouts.

10.4Guarantee Release on CoC

AMC’s guarantee is automatically released upon a Change of Control where AMC ceases to hold 50%+ of the Company’s voting power or upon a sale of substantially all assets. CoC simultaneously triggers the put right AND releases AMC’s guarantee.

15. Amendment Provisions — Sacred Rights & Blocking Thresholds (Section 9.02)
15.1Majority Consent (>50%)

The Indenture, Notes, Guarantees, ICA, and Security Documents may be modified with consent of at least a majority in aggregate principal of outstanding Notes.

15.2Sacred Rights — Each Affected Holder Must Consent

Without consent of each affected Holder, no amendment may:

(a) Change Maturity Date, interest payment timing, reduce principal, reduce interest rate, reduce redemption premium, or change payment currency;

(b) Reduce/change/impair the Change of Control Payment (101%);

(c) Amend right to sue for enforcement of payment on/after stated maturity or redemption date;

(d) Reduce the percentage threshold for amendments/waivers;

(e) Reduce obligation to pay Additional Amounts or tax indemnity;

(f) Modify Section 9.02 itself, or Sections 6.04/6.05 (except to increase required percentages).

15.3Super-Majority — 66 2/3% for Collateral Release

Without consent of at least 66 2/3%, no amendment may: - Release Liens on all or substantially all Collateral; or - Change/alter priority of Liens on any material portion of Collateral in a manner materially adverse to Holders

(Unless already permitted by the Indenture or Security Documents.)

15.4Deemed Consent for ICA/Security Documents

Holders are deemed to consent to amendments adding additional secured parties, so long as additional liens are permitted under the Indenture. No separate Holder vote needed.

15.5Covenant Waiver by Majority (Section 4.15)

Majority Holders may waive compliance with Sections 4.03-4.12, 4.13(a), and 4.17 in any particular instance. All major covenants (debt incurrence, restricted payments, asset sales, change of control, affiliate transactions, liens) can be waived by a simple majority for a specific transaction.

17. Affiliate Transaction Restrictions (Section 4.09)
17.1General Rule

No transactions with Affiliates (including AMC) except on terms substantially as favorable as arm’s-length.

17.2Thresholds & Exceptions
Threshold Requirement
Below greater of $6M and 3% of EBITDA No restrictions — no arm’s-length test needed
Above threshold Arm’s-length terms required (“substantially as favorable”)
17.3Key Exempt Transactions
  • Transactions between Company and Restricted Subsidiaries (intra-group)
  • Tax sharing/grouping payments (must comply with Section 4.07)
  • Parent Expenses and customary fees to Parent Entity directors/officers
  • Agreements existing at Issue Date (or amendments not materially adverse)

  • Advisory/investment banking fees approved by majority of Board

  • Loans/investments permitted under the Indenture

18.2The AMC-Odeon Linkage Problem

The single most critical issue for Odeon noteholders: AMC bankruptcy = automatic Odeon Event of Default (Section 6.01(e)/(f)). This means:

  1. AMC’s US restructuring directly threatens Odeon’s stability even if European operations are healthy
  2. Upon acceleration, the call premium becomes due (currently ~$12.8M; drops to zero after Nov 2026)
  3. Holders can rescind acceleration if majority agrees, but this requires coordinated action
  4. The guarantee release on Change of Control means a sale of Odeon by AMC would simultaneously trigger the put right AND eliminate the AMC backstop
18.3Strategic Scenarios for Odeon Noteholders

Scenario A: AMC Refinances Before Nov 2027 (Best Case) - AMC calls Odeon Notes at par (after Nov 2026) or 103.188% (before) - Noteholders receive full par + any applicable premium - Strong incentive for AMC given the 12.75% coupon ($51M/year cash interest)

Scenario B: AMC Files Ch.11 Before Nov 2026 - Automatic acceleration at Odeon - Premium of ~103.188% becomes due - European ring-fence may protect Odeon operations but legal linkage creates uncertainty - Majority Holders can rescind acceleration if Odeon itself is performing

Scenario C: AMC Files Ch.11 After Nov 2026 - Automatic acceleration at par (no premium) - Same ring-fence / rescission dynamics - More favorable for the company, less for Holders

Scenario D: AMC Sells Odeon - Change of Control triggered → 101% put right - 90% drag-along means near-certain tender - AMC guarantee simultaneously released - Buyers may seek to refinance at lower coupon

Scenario E: Distressed Exchange / LME at Odeon Level - Given above-par trading and approaching maturity, less likely - Amendment requires only 50%+1 for covenant waivers - Sacred rights protect economic terms - 66 2/3% needed to release collateral

18.4Defensive Playbook for Odeon Noteholders
  1. Accumulate 33 1/3%+ position — blocks collateral release / lien subordination
  2. Monitor AMC bankruptcy risk — the automatic cross-default is the primary threat
  3. Watch Parent Expenses — uncapped management fees/advisory costs could leak value upstream to AMC
  4. Track affiliate transactions below $6M/3% EBITDA — no arm’s-length requirement, no approval needed
  5. Coordinate with other Holders — form cooperation agreement to control Instructing Group threshold
  6. Consider rescission strategy — if AMC files Ch.11, majority Holders can rescind Odeon acceleration to preserve European ring-fence value
  7. Monitor call economics — after Nov 2026, AMC can call at par; the 12.75% coupon makes refinancing highly attractive
